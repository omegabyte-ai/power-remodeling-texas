Material Palette

What your cabinets are made of matters.

We work with a curated selection of wood species and finish options — chosen for durability, grain character, and compatibility with Houston's climate.

Maple

Fine, consistent grain that takes paint beautifully. The standard for painted cabinetry because it produces the smoothest surface with minimal grain show-through. Hard, dense, and highly durable under daily use.

White Oak

Currently the most-requested species for natural stain finishes. Wide, pronounced grain with a warm undertone. Takes wire brushing and light cerusing beautifully for a contemporary European aesthetic.

Alder

Lighter and more uniform than oak, alder accepts stain evenly and works well for transitional and farmhouse styles. Cost-effective option for full-custom projects where budget matters but quality doesn't.

Walnut

Rich chocolate tones and dramatic grain — walnut is the premium choice for high-end kitchen islands, study libraries, and executive home offices. Typically specified for accent pieces and island bases alongside painted perimeter cabinetry.

Cabinet Refacing

New look. Half the price.

If your cabinet boxes are structurally sound but the doors, drawers, and hardware are dated — refacing is a smart alternative to full replacement.

Refacing replaces your door and drawer fronts, applies matching veneer to the visible box faces, and installs all new hardware and soft-close mechanisms. The result is indistinguishable from new cabinetry — at roughly 40–60% of the cost. We offer this where it makes sense, and full replacement where it doesn't. We'll tell you the difference honestly.
