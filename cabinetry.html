Material Palette

What your cabinets are made of matters.

We work with a curated selection of wood species and finish options — chosen for durability, grain character, and compatibility with Houston's climate.

Maple

Fine, consistent grain that takes paint beautifully. The standard for painted cabinetry because it produces the smoothest surface with minimal grain show-through. Hard, dense, and highly durable under daily use.

White Oak

Currently the most-requested species for natural stain finishes. Wide, pronounced grain with a warm undertone. Takes wire brushing and light cerusing beautifully for a contemporary European aesthetic.

Alder

Lighter and more uniform than oak, alder accepts stain evenly and works well for transitional and farmhouse styles. Cost-effective option for full-custom projects where budget matters but quality doesn't.

Walnut

Rich chocolate tones and dramatic grain — walnut is the premium choice for high-end kitchen islands, study libraries, and executive home offices. Typically specified for accent pieces and island bases alongside painted perimeter cabinetry.

Cabinet Refacing

New look. Half the price.

If your cabinet boxes are structurally sound but the doors, drawers, and hardware are dated — refacing is a smart alternative to full replacement.

Refacing replaces your door and drawer fronts, applies matching veneer to the visible box faces, and installs all new hardware and soft-close mechanisms. The result is indistinguishable from new cabinetry — at roughly 40–60% of the cost. We offer this where it makes sense, and full replacement where it doesn't. We'll tell you the difference honestly.

High-End Details

The details that separate good cabinetry from unforgettable cabinetry.

Homeowners who have lived with beautifully built cabinetry know the difference. Here's what we specify on every high-end project — details that are invisible until you live with them.

Custom In-Drawer Organizer Systems

Pull-out drawer inserts fitted precisely to your drawer dimensions — individually partitioned for cutlery, utensils, spice jars, and knives. Solid walnut or maple dividers (not plastic), soft-close undermount slides, and compartment sizing based on what you actually own. We measure your existing cookware before we spec a single compartment.

Available in knife block integration, hidden drawer-in-drawer configurations, and full-extension designs that bring the back of the drawer to you. The difference between a drawer that works and one you'll use for 20 years.

LED Under-Cabinet & Toe-Kick Lighting

Warm-white LED strips recessed beneath upper cabinet bottoms and inside toe-kick channels — wired directly to a dimmer, not plug-in strips. Provides task lighting over the entire countertop surface without a single visible fixture. At night, the ambient glow from the toe-kick defines the kitchen without overhead lighting.

We also wire interior cabinet lighting for glass-front uppers and pantry towers — motion-activated, so the light turns on when you open the door and off when you close it.

Waterfall Marble & Quartz Islands

The waterfall edge — where the countertop continues vertically down the side of the island to the floor — is the signature detail of a high-end kitchen. We spec book-matched Calacatta Gold or Viola marble (veining aligned across the seam), or matching engineered quartz for zero-maintenance performance. The island cabinet is built with an inset frame specifically to receive the waterfall panel.

Combined with toe-kick LED lighting beneath, this creates the floating island effect you see in architectural photography — achievable in your home.

Fluted & Reeded Cabinet Faces

Vertical reeding on island panels, vanity faces, and lower cabinet fronts adds dimensional texture that flat slab doors can't deliver. Available in painted MDF for a clean contemporary look, or in white oak with a wire-brushed finish for warmth. Reeded glass inserts for upper cabinet doors soften the kitchen while keeping dishware visible.

This is the 2026 detail replacing shiplap as the dominant interior texture in Texas luxury remodeling. We mill and install it in-house.
