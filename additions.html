Before We Break Ground

The planning phase is where additions succeed — or fail.

Most addition problems start before a single shovel hits the ground. We invest heavily upfront in design, engineering, and site analysis so nothing surprises us — or you — once construction begins.

What makes a great addition plan?

The difference between an addition that blends seamlessly and one that looks tacked on is almost entirely a planning problem — not a construction one. Roofline continuity, exterior material matching, structural integration, and MEP coordination all happen in planning. We get this right, which is why our additions come in on time, on budget, and look like they've always been there.

  • 01
    Site Analysis & Setback Review
    We verify your lot's build envelope, setback requirements, and HOA restrictions before designing anything — no false starts.
  • 02
    Structural Engineering
    Licensed PE signs off on foundation, beam sizing, and load paths. Required for permits. We include this — it's not an add-on charge.
  • 03
    Utility Integration Plan
    HVAC capacity, electrical panel loads, gas routing, and drainage — all mapped before construction. No mid-project surprises.
  • 04
    Exterior Match Strategy
    Roofline pitch, siding profile, window style, brick and stone matching. We source materials that disappear into the existing home.

Our Process

From consultation to move-in. No guesswork.

Additions involve structural, mechanical, architectural, and finish work converging on a single deadline. Our process keeps everything coordinated and keeps you informed throughout.

01

Design & Planning

We meet on-site, review your vision, assess structural feasibility, and produce architectural drawings and a fixed-price contract. Engineering, permit drawings, and HOA submittals are all included — not billed separately.

02

Permits & Approvals

We manage the full permit process with city or county building departments and your HOA. Permit timelines vary by municipality — we give you a realistic schedule and handle every inspection as it comes.

03

Construction

Foundation, framing, roofing, windows, exterior finish, MEP rough-in, insulation, drywall, and all interior finishes — self-performed or closely managed by our crew. Weekly meetings and daily photo updates. We minimize disruption to your occupied living spaces throughout.

04

Final Inspection & CO

We coordinate final city inspection, obtain the Certificate of Occupancy, and walk the completed addition with you. Every punch list item resolved before final payment. Your new space is move-in ready — completely, not approximately.
